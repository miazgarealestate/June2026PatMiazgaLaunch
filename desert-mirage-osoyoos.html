The Community

What Desert Mirage actually is — and who buys here

Desert Mirage is a resort strata complex in central Osoyoos, built in a Spanish Mission style that suits the desert landscape well. Concrete construction, resort landscaping, two outdoor pools, and hot tub — plus the central Osoyoos location that puts you within walking distance of the lake, restaurants, and the town centre.

The buyer profile at Desert Mirage skews toward people who want a combination of personal use and rental income. The central location and resort amenities make it attractive for short-term vacation rental guests — Osoyoos draws significant summer tourism, and a well-managed condo in a resort complex can generate meaningful rental revenue during peak season.

Units range from studios to two-bedroom configurations. Pricing varies significantly by floor, view, and unit size — lakeside or lakeview units command premiums. Interior-facing units offer better value for buyers primarily focused on rental income rather than personal-use lifestyle.

⚠️ STR rules — always verify before buying for rental income

Desert Mirage has historically been regarded as STR-friendly — but short-term rental permissions are set by strata bylaws, and bylaws can change. The Town of Osoyoos also introduced an STR principal residence requirement that applies within town limits.

Before purchasing Desert Mirage for rental income purposes, verify: (1) the current strata bylaws on STR permissions, (2) any minimum stay requirements, (3) the Town of Osoyoos STR licensing rules current at time of purchase, and (4) whether the unit qualifies under applicable rules.

What to Know Before Buying

Desert Mirage purchase considerations

📄 Strata Documents

Review depreciation report, strata financials, bylaws, and meeting minutes before any offer. Pay particular attention to the reserve fund — resort strata pools and amenities require ongoing capital expenditure.

💰 Strata Fees

Resort amenities cost more to maintain than standard residential strata. Factor strata fees into your rental income calculations — they are a fixed cost regardless of occupancy.

🏊 Amenity Maintenance

Pools, hot tubs, and resort landscaping require regular capital outlay. A depreciation report will tell you the state of the reserve fund and whether special levies are likely on the horizon.

🔥 Wildfire Insurance

Get insurance confirmed as a subject condition — especially if purchasing in summer months. Insurers won't bind new policies within ~50km of an active wildfire. The strata carries building insurance; your personal condo policy covers contents and improvements.

📍 Unit Selection

Location within the complex matters. Higher floors, lake-facing, and pool-adjacent units command premiums and typically outperform on rental income. Ground floor or interior units offer better entry price but different rental dynamics.

🤝 Property Management

If purchasing for STR without being primarily resident, you'll need a property management company for guest turnover, cleaning, and maintenance. Factor management fees (typically 20–30% of gross) into your income projections.
