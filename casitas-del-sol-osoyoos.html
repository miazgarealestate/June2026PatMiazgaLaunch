Who Casitas Del Sol Is For

Retirement lifestyle without the resort premium

Casitas Del Sol attracts buyers who have done the math on retirement in the South Okanagan and want quality of life without paying resort-community prices. The 55+ designation creates a community of people at a similar life stage — quieter, more settled, and oriented around the lifestyle of the valley rather than the transient resort scene.

The rancher-style homes — single level, 995–1,495 sq ft — are designed for low-maintenance living. No stairs. Easy upkeep. A lock-and-leave lifestyle that appeals to buyers who split time between Osoyoos and their home province, or who are making this their full-time permanent residence.

The location on the hillside above town means lake and valley views from most units. The golf course adjacency is a genuine daily-life advantage — you don't need a car to play a round. And the strata fees, at around $120/month, are low by resort-community standards — a meaningful factor in total cost of ownership calculations.

What Buyers Need to Know

Buying into Casitas Del Sol — the important details

👴 The 55+ Rule

The age restriction means at least one occupant must be 55 or older. Verify how the bylaw is written — some communities require all occupants to be 55+, others allow one qualifying resident. Always confirm with the current strata documents.

📄 Strata Documents

Review the depreciation report, strata minutes, bylaws, and insurance policy before making any offer. The reserve fund status tells you whether a special levy is likely — and in resort-adjacent communities, deferred maintenance can be expensive.

🏠 Rental Rules

Many 55+ strata communities have restrictions on rentals — minimum rental terms, tenant age requirements, or outright rental restrictions. If rental income is part of your plan, verify this explicitly before purchasing.

💰 Total Cost of Ownership

Strata fees, BC property taxes, insurance, and any utilities not covered by strata. The $120/mo strata fee is low by South Okanagan resort standards — but always confirm the current rate and what it covers before budgeting.

🔍 Pat's Assessment

Pat's construction background is particularly useful on rancher-style homes — single-level construction means the envelope, roof, and foundation are the primary structural considerations. He knows what to look for on properties of this era and style.

📍 Lock-and-Leave

The gated community, low-maintenance design, and strata management make Casitas Del Sol well-suited for part-time residents — buyers who plan to be here seasonally and want peace of mind when they're not.
